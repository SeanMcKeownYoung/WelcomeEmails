Good morning, Michigan

Across the state, residents have been dealing with numerous issues as communities face widespread flooding, tornadoes, and straight-line winds. Roads, homes, and property have all been damaged as a result.

 

Gov. Gretchen Whitmer issued a state of emergency in 32 counties on Wednesday, five days after issuing one in Cheboygan County. She declared a state of emergency to five more counties on Friday, bringing the total under a declaration statewide to 38.

 

The American Red Cross announced that four emergency shelters have been opened in Northern Michigan for those impacted by ongoing flooding.

Why food prices are squeezing Michigan households despite cooling inflation

Rachel Bawks remembers when $350 every two weeks was enough to feed her family of six. Grocery prices would rise and fall, but over the past two years, she said, it seems like food costs haven’t come back down. These days, that grocery bill is closer to $650-$700.

 

Bawks is one of dozens of MLive readers who responded this past week to our question about which bill or cost is causing your household the most financial stress right now.

 

Grocery prices in the U.S. are expected to increase by 3.1% in 2026, according to predictions from the U.S. Department of Agriculture. That’s higher than the historical average pace of growth, which is 2.6%.

Michigan judges often recommend slashing utility profits. It rarely happens

When a Michigan judge recommended a major cut to Consumers Energy’s profits this year, utility bigwigs didn’t sweat it.

 

Consumers CEO Garrick Rochow told investors he wasn’t concerned “at all” on a February call. He predicted state regulators would ignore the judge, maintaining or even increasing a key metric that influences how much utility customers pay for shareholder profit via their power bills.

 

In March, while granting Consumers the largest electric rate hike in decades, they proved him right. They often do .

 

For years, appointed utility regulators in Michigan have routinely rejected the nonbinding decisions of administrative law judges who find the allowed rate of utility profit too high, even after reviewing the same evidence. The judges play the role of referee in annual utility rate proceedings but don’t determine the final score.

Michigan claims first wheelchair tennis national championship

The University of Michigan captured its first Intercollegiate Tennis Association (ITA) National Wheelchair Tennis Championship on Sunday, April 12, defeating defending champion Alabama at the United States Tennis Association (USTA) National Campus in Orlando, Florida.

 

The top-seeded Wolverines swept through pool play without dropping a point, defeating Cincinnati and Houston before advancing past Auburn 2-0 in the semifinals.

 

Michigan clinched the championship title by winning the doubles point and splitting singles matches against Alabama.

Detroit unveils 6-point summer safety plan after teen takeover chaos

Detroit officials on April 16 unveiled a new summer safety plan aimed at curbing youth violence and large gatherings downtown, following a series of chaotic “teen takeovers.”

 

Mayor Mary Sheffield and Police Chief Todd Bettison announced the six-point plan Thursday, emphasizing a mix of enforcement and youth engagement strategies as the city prepares for warmer months.

 

The plan comes after multiple recent incidents downtown, including a disturbance the night of April 11 when large crowds of teens flooded the area just hours after city leaders met with youth organizers to promote safer alternatives.
